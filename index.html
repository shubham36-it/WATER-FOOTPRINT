ABOUT US

JAL MITRA is a powerful tool for assessing water use in agriculture and industries. By understanding the water footprint of different products and processes, we can identify opportunities for water savings and promote more sustainable water use practices in agriculture..

CROPS-RICE

The global water footprint for rice production is approximately 784 km³ per year, with an average water footprint of 1325 m³ per ton of rice produced. This footprint consists of different components: about 48% is attributed to green water

INDUSTRIES CHOCLATE

WATER FOOTPRINT of chocolate is quite substantial, with an average of 1700 liters of water required to produce a 100 gram chocolate bar. This equates to approximately 450 gallons of water per 3.5 ounces of chocolate.

SIDE PRODUCTS

Cotton seed oil: When cotton is grown for fiber, cottonseed oil is a valuable by-product. The water footprint of cottonseed oil is estimated to be around 2.1 m³/kg
